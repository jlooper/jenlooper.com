# Impeccable — project design context

This file was created by **impeccable teach**. Agents should read it before doing design or frontend work on this site.

## Design Context

### Users

- **Primary audience**: Developers learning from your content—articles, curricula, projects, and speaking-adjacent material.
- **Context**: Self-directed learning, skimming for ideas, and longer reads when something clicks.
- **Job to be done**: Understand concepts and **leave inspired to try new things**, trusting guidance from someone who blends disciplines (tech, education, writing, creative practice).
- **Emotional goal**: Curiosity, permission to experiment, and confidence that “serious” content can still feel human and creative—not homework, not hype.

### Brand Personality

- **Three words**: **Interdisciplinary**, **creative**, **experimental**.
- **Voice**: Expert without being stiff; warm but not saccharine; credible for devs without sliding into corporate “thought leadership” gloss.
- **Positioning**: You are the guide who connects dots across fields; the interface should feel like a thoughtful studio or editorial publication, not a SaaS marketing site.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **References** (mood and structure, not clones):
  - [Stéphanie Walter’s site](https://stephaniewalter.design/): clear hierarchy, strong personal framing, accessibility-forward craft, content blocks that breathe.
  - [The Marginalian](https://www.themarginalian.org/): **minimalism and focus**—clean layouts that privilege long-form reading; **editorial typography** (strong serif or refined sans for sustained reading).
- **Aspirational techniques** (use where they genuinely aid the story, not as decoration):
  - **Scrollytelling & motion**: scroll-driven progression for key narratives (inspiration: rich editorial / spatial pieces—only when it supports comprehension).
  - **Data-driven visuals**: charts, diagrams, or spatial/interactive explanations where they clarify technical or quantitative ideas.
  - **High-contrast schemes**: respect readability and accessibility; dark surfaces with disciplined, **non-neon** accents can feel modern and serious without “cyber” cliché.
- **Theme**: **Light and dark modes** with a **user-controlled toggle** (not light-only). Defaults should still feel inviting in light mode; dark mode should remain readable and calm.
- **Anti-references**:
  - Generic **corporate** polish (anonymous hero metrics, interchangeable card grids, stock “innovation” imagery).
  - The **purple gradient / cyan-on-dark “AI product”** aesthetic and other template-y “slop” palettes.

### Design Principles

1. **Content over spectacle**: Layout and motion serve reading and learning; if an effect does not clarify or motivate, omit it.
2. **Editorial clarity**: Typography and spacing should support long sessions at the screen; hierarchy beats decoration.
3. **Interdisciplinary signal**: Visual and interaction choices can be bold or playful when they reinforce *your* voice—experimentation is on-brand when it is intentional.
4. **Accessible contrast by default**: Favor legible pairings in both themes; high contrast is a feature, not an afterthought. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for motion-heavy ideas.
5. **No faux-corporate, no faux-futurism**: Avoid palettes and patterns associated with generic SaaS or generic “AI” branding; prefer warm, human, or sharply editorial alternatives.
6. **Evolving craft**: Redesigns may refresh type, color tokens, and components; preserve **strategic** warmth and personality rather than any single font or hue by default.
